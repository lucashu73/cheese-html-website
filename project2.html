    • Semi-soft Cheeses

    These cheeses aren't hard, but they're not that soft either. They have a high moisture content and usually have a mild-taste. Well-known varieties include Havarti, Muenster and Port Salut.

    Havarti

    One of my favorite cheeses to put on sandwiches and Denmark's most famous cheese. A truly delicious, mild and creamy semi-soft cheese! Available with various different flavours, my favorite is Jalapeno Havarti. Picture shown on left.

    Muenster

    This one sort of rides on the line between soft and semi-soft cheese. I have to admit that I've only used this cheese once to make macaroni. But it was delicious! A savory and sharp taste, best served in dishes such as; grilled sandwiches, macaroni and cheese, pizza and cheeseburgers. I once had a dream of this hideous amalgamation of cheese, it must've been a Muenster! And yes, it's pronounced like mun-ster. Picture shown in middle.

    Port Salut

    Yet again another French cheese. I have also not tried this one yet and don't have much to say about it. I do know that it's one of those cheeses served with crackers and meats on those charcuterie boards. So, it's probably too fancy for me anyways. I mean...even the name sounds fancy. Picture shown on right.
